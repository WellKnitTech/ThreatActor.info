## Description

Adversaries may move onto devices by exploiting or copying malware to devices connected via USB. In the case of Lateral Movement, adversaries may utilize the physical connection of a device to a compromised or malicious charging station or PC to bypass application store requirements and install malicious applications directly.[Lau-Mactans](https://media.blackhat.com/us-13/US-13-Lau-Mactans-Injecting-Malware-into-iOS-Devices-via-Malicious-Chargers-WP.pdf) In the case of Initial Access, adversaries may attempt to exploit the device via the connection to gain access to data stored on the device.[Krebs-JuiceJacking](http://krebsonsecurity.com/2011/08/beware-of-juice-jacking/) Examples of this include: 
 
* Exploiting insecure bootloaders in a Nexus 6 or 6P device over USB and gaining the ability to perform actions including intercepting phone calls, intercepting network traffic, and obtaining the device physical location.[IBM-NexusUSB](https://securityintelligence.com/android-vulnerabilities-attacking-nexus-6-and-6p-custom-boot-modes/) 
* Exploiting weakly-enforced security boundaries in Android devices such as the Google Pixel 2 over USB.[GoogleProjectZero-OATmeal](https://googleprojectzero.blogspot.com/2018/09/oatmeal-on-universal-cereal-bus.html) 
* Products from Cellebrite and Grayshift purportedly that can exploit some iOS devices using physical access to the data port to unlock the passcode.[Computerworld-iPhoneCracking](https://www.techcentral.ie/two-vendors-now-sell-iphone-cracking-technology-police-buying/)
