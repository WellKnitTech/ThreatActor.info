## Description

Adversaries may leverage messaging services for SMS pumping, which may impact system and/or hosted service availability. [Twilio SMS Pumping](https://www.twilio.com/en-us/blog/sms-pumping-fraud-solutions) SMS pumping is a type of telecommunications fraud whereby a threat actor first obtains a set of phone numbers from a telecommunications provider, then leverages a victim’s messaging infrastructure to send large amounts of SMS messages to numbers in that set. By generating SMS traffic to their phone number set, a threat actor may earn payments from the telecommunications provider. [Twilio SMS Pumping Fraud](https://www.twilio.com/docs/glossary/what-is-sms-pumping-fraud)

Threat actors often use publicly available web forms, such as one-time password (OTP) or account verification fields, in order to generate SMS traffic. These fields may leverage services such as Twilio, AWS SNS, and Amazon Cognito in the background. [Twilio SMS Pumping](https://www.twilio.com/en-us/blog/sms-pumping-fraud-solutions) [AWS RE:Inforce Threat Detection 2024](https://reinforce.awsevents.com/content/dam/reinforce/2024/slides/TDR432_New-tactics-and-techniques-for-proactive-threat-detection.pdf) In response to the large quantity of requests, SMS costs may increase and communication channels may become overwhelmed. [Twilio SMS Pumping](https://www.twilio.com/en-us/blog/sms-pumping-fraud-solutions)
