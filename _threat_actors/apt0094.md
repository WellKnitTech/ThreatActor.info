## Introduction
Kimsuky (also known as APT43, Velvet Chollima, THALLIUM, or Emerald Sleet) is a North Korea-based cyber espionage group that has been active since at least 2012. The group initially targeted South Korean government agencies, think tanks, and subject-matter experts. Its operations expanded to include the United Nations and organizations in the government, education, business services, and manufacturing sectors across the United States, Japan, Russia, and Europe.

Kimsuky focuses on collection related to foreign policy and national security issues tied to the Korean Peninsula, nuclear policy, and sanctions. In 2023, the group was observed using commercial large language models to assist with vulnerability research, scripting, social engineering, and reconnaissance.

## Notable Campaigns
1. **Korea Hydro & Nuclear Power Co. (2014)**: Attack on South Korean nuclear power operator
2. **Operation STOLEN PENCIL (2018)**: Targeting academic institutions
3. **Operation Kabar Cobra (2019)**: Campaign against government entities
4. **Operation Smoke Screen (2019)**: Campaign using decoy documents
5. **Campaign Targeting UN Security Council (2019-2020)**: Targeting UN officials

## Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures (TTPs)
Kimsuky is known for the following TTPs:

### Initial Access
- **Spear Phishing**: Malicious attachments (CHM, HWP, Word documents)
- **Phishing**: Credential harvesting emails
- **Drive-by Compromise**: Watering hole attacks
- ** Torrents**: Distributing malware through torrent sites

### Execution
- **PowerShell**: Using PowerShell for execution
- **Windows Command Shell**: Using cmd.exe
- **Scripting**: VBScript and JavaScript execution

### Persistence
- **Malicious browser extensions**: Installing browser extensions
- **Scheduled Tasks**: Creating scheduled tasks

### Defense Evasion
- **Obfuscation**: Using encoded scripts
- **Phishing**: Using spoofed domains and personas

### Discovery
- **System Information Discovery**
- **File and Directory Discovery**

### Collection
- **Input Capture**: Keylogging
- **Screen Capture**
- **Clipboard Data**

### Exfiltration
- **Exfiltration Over C2 Channel**

### Resource Development
- **Tool Purchasing**: Using cover identities to purchase tools
