## Introduction
Sandworm Team (also known as APT44, Voodoo Bear, or ELECTRUM) is a destructive threat group that has been attributed to Russia's General Staff Main Intelligence Directorate (GRU) Main Center for Special Technologies (GTsST) military unit 74455. Active since at least 2009, Sandworm is one of the most dangerous threat groups in the world due to its demonstrated capability to attack operational technology (OT) systems directly, including industrial control systems, circuit breakers, and SCADA infrastructure.

The group is known for its destructive attacks including the 2015 and 2016 attacks against Ukrainian electrical companies, the 2017 worldwide NotPetya attack, and the 2018 Olympic Destroyer attack against the Winter Olympic Games.

## Notable Campaigns
1. **Ukrainian Power Grid Attacks (2015-2016)**: Coordinated attacks causing power outages affecting hundreds of thousands of customers
2. **NotPetya Worldwide Attack (2017)**: Global destructive wiper attack causing billions in damages
3. **Olympic Destroyer (2018)**: Attack on Pyeongchang Winter Olympic Games opening ceremony
4. **French Presidential Campaign (2017)**: Targeting of the 2017 French presidential campaign
5. **Georgian Attacks (2018-2019)**: Large-scale cyberattacks against Georgian organizations

## Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures (TTPs)
Sandworm is known for the following TTPs:

### Initial Access
- **Spear Phishing**: Targeting specific individuals with malicious documents
- **Supply Chain Compromise**: Compromising software vendors (e.g., M.E.Doc for NotPetya)
- **Exploit Public-Facing Applications**: Exploiting vulnerabilities in EXIM mail server and other services

### Execution
- **Scripting**: Extensive use of PowerShell for execution
- **Windows Management Instrumentation (WMI)**: For system discovery and execution
- **Scheduled Tasks**: Creating scheduled tasks for persistence

### Persistence
- **Account Manipulation**: Using legitimate credentials for persistence
- **Scheduled Tasks**: Creating scheduled tasks for ongoing access

### Defense Evasion
- **Obfuscation**: Using algorithm to obscure malware features
- **Clear Logs**: Deleting event logs to obstruct investigation
- **Rootkits**: Using kernel-level rootkits

### Discovery
- **System Information Discovery**: Using PowerShell to enumerate systems
- **Network Service Discovery**: Identifying connected systems

### Lateral Movement
- **Remote Services**: Exploiting remote services for lateral movement
- **PsExec**: Using PsExec for remote execution

### Impact
- **Data Encrypted for Impact**: Using wiper malware to destroy data
- **System Shutdown**: Causing power grid outages

### ICS/OT-Specific Techniques
- **Industrial Control Protocol Manipulation**: Using Industroyer to manipulate IEC 60870-5-104 protocols
- **Denial of Service**: Causing grid outages

## Malware and Tools
Sandworm uses a range of custom malware:

### Destructive Malware
- **BlackEnergy**: Early malware with killdisk capability
- **NotPetya**: Global wiper disguised as ransomware
- **Olympic Destroyer**: Wiper targeting Olympic Games
- **CaddyWiper**: Destructive wiper for Ukraine operations
- **WhisperGate/HermeticWiper**: 2022 Ukraine conflict wipers

### ICS/OT-Specific Malware
- **Industroyer (2016)**: First malware to directly speak industrial control protocols
- **Industroyer2 (2022)**: Next-gen variant with Linux worm integration

### Botnets
- **VPNFilter**: Compromised 600,000+ SOHO routers
- **Cyclops Blink**: VPNFilter successor targeting ASUS routers

### Backdoors
- **KillDisk**: Data destruction capability
- **TANKTRAP**: PowerShell utility for GPO distribution
- **CredRaptor**: Browser credential harvester
- **plainpwd**: Mimikatz-derived credential dumper

## Attribution and Evidence
Sandworm Team has been attributed to Russia's GRU military unit 74455. In October 2020, the US indicted six GRU officers associated with this group for various cyber operations. The group has collaborated with APT28 (GRU Unit 26165) on some operations.

### Government Affiliation
- Russian General Staff Main Intelligence Directorate (GRU)
- Military Unit 74455 (Main Center for Special Technologies)

### Linked Groups
