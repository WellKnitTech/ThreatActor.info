## Introduction

MuddyWater is an Iranian cyber espionage cluster assessed as a subordinate element of the Ministry of Intelligence and Security (MOIS). The group has been publicly tracked since at least 2017 and has repeatedly targeted government and commercial organizations across the Middle East, Asia, Africa, Europe, and North America.

Public reporting consistently links MuddyWater to credential theft, long-term access, and post-compromise collection rather than smash-and-grab disruption. Its operations commonly blend spearphishing, abuse of legitimate administration tools, and custom PowerShell- or script-based malware families that are frequently repackaged over time.

## Activities and Tactics

- **Primary objective:** espionage, credential access, and long-term foothold establishment in government and enterprise environments.
- **Target profile:** telecommunications, local government, defense, oil and gas, and other organizations with regional political or strategic relevance.
- **Geographic focus:** repeated activity against Middle Eastern targets, with additional victimology spanning Europe, Africa, Asia, and North America.
- **Access methods:** spearphishing attachments and links, exploitation of public-facing vulnerabilities, and DLL side-loading through trusted binaries.
- **Common tradecraft:** PowerShell execution, Windows scripting, persistence through Run keys and scheduled tasks, staged delivery, credential dumping, and use of remote administration tools.

## Notable Campaigns

- **2017 Middle East spearphishing and POWERSTATS activity:** Unit 42 tied a wide set of regionally tailored lure documents and shared infrastructure to the cluster it named MuddyWater, highlighting repeated use of POWERSTATS, open-source offensive tooling, and compromised third-party accounts in delivery.
- **2018-2019 Lebanon, Oman, and broader regional follow-on activity:** ClearSky and other vendors documented continued operations using updated implants, obfuscated scripts, and exploit-enabled delivery against regional government and telecom targets.
- **Operation Quicksand:** later reporting linked MuddyWater to phishing and loader activity that expanded beyond the group's earlier document-lure patterns while maintaining its espionage focus.
- **2022 joint advisory reporting:** CISA, FBI, CNMF, and NCSC-UK described MuddyWater as an Iranian government-sponsored actor using malware families such as PowGoop, Small Sieve, Mori, Canopy/STARWHALE, and POWERSTATS in global government and commercial intrusions.

## Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures (TTPs)

- **Spearphishing:** malicious attachments and links delivered through tailored regional lures and compromised third-party accounts.
- **Script-heavy execution:** PowerShell, VBScript, JavaScript, Python, and Windows command shell abuse, often with heavy obfuscation.
- **Persistence:** Registry Run keys, scheduled tasks, Office template macros, and DLL side-loading.
- **Credential access and discovery:** use of tools such as LaZagne, Mimikatz, and CrackMapExec alongside broad host, user, and network enumeration.
- **Command and control:** HTTP(S), web services, multi-stage channels, proxy infrastructure, and use of legitimate remote management tooling such as ScreenConnect.
- **Defense evasion:** Base64 and custom command obfuscation, trusted binary proxy execution (`mshta.exe`, `rundll32.exe`, `cmstp.exe`), and masquerading around Microsoft- or Defender-themed filenames.

## Malware and Tools

- **POWERSTATS:** long-running MuddyWater PowerShell backdoor family used for staged execution and remote tasking.
- **PowGoop:** loader and PowerShell-based downloader family highlighted in the 2022 joint advisory.
- **STARWHALE / Canopy:** scripted malware used for persistence, host discovery, and exfiltration-related tasking.
- **Mori:** backdoor family associated with DNS- and HTTP-based command and control behavior.
- **Small Sieve:** Python backdoor delivered via NSIS installer and Telegram-based tasking.
- **PhonyC2 / MuddyC2Go / DCHSpy:** later associated implants and supporting tooling tied to evolving MuddyWater operations.
- **LaZagne / CrackMapExec / ScreenConnect:** legitimate or publicly available tools repeatedly observed in credential access, lateral movement, and remote operations.

## Attribution and Evidence

Multiple public sources assess MuddyWater as operating on behalf of Iran, with U.S. government reporting specifically describing the group as a subordinate element within MOIS. That assessment is reinforced by the group's long-running regional targeting, infrastructure patterns, and malware/tooling overlap across vendor and government reporting.

Attribution should still be read as a confidence-based assessment rather than a courtroom finding. Vendor naming varies significantly across the ecosystem, which is why aliases such as Earth Vetala, MERCURY, Seedworm, Static Kitten, and Mango Sandstorm remain important for cross-referencing public reporting.
