## Introduction
MuddyWater (also known as Seedworm, Mango Sandstorm, or MERCURY) is a cyber espionage group assessed to be a subordinate element within Iran's Ministry of Intelligence and Security (MOIS). Since at least 2017, MuddyWater has targeted a range of government and private organizations across sectors including telecommunications, local government, defense, and oil and natural gas organizations, in the Middle East, Asia, Africa, Europe, and North America.

## Activities and Tactics
MuddyWater conducts targeted cyber espionage:

### Primary Targets
- Telecommunications organizations
- Local government agencies
- Defense contractors
- Oil and natural gas organizations
- Various private sector companies

### Key Motivations
- Intelligence gathering
- Strategic access
- Data theft

### Geographic Focus
- Middle East
- Asia
- Africa
- Europe
- North America

## Notable Campaigns
1. **Regional Targeting**: Middle East-focused operations
2. **Telecommunications Attacks**: Compromising telecom providers
3. **Energy Sector Operations**: Targeting oil and gas organizations

## Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures (TTPs)
MuddyWater is known for the following TTPs:

### Initial Access
- **Spear Phishing**: Targeted email campaigns
- **Watering Hole Attacks**: Compromising legitimate sites

### Execution
- **Command and Scripting Interpreter**
- **PowerShell**
- **VBScript**

### Persistence
- **Scheduled Tasks**
- **Registry Keys**
- **WMI Event Subscription**

### Defense Evasion
- **Obfuscation**
- **Disable Security Tools**

### Discovery
- **System Information Discovery**
- **Account Discovery**

### Lateral Movement
- **Remote Services**
- **Windows Admin Shares**

### Collection
- **Screen Capture**
- **Keylogging**

### Exfiltration
- **Exfiltration Over C2 Channel**
