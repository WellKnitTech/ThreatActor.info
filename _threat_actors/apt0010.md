## Introduction
Turla (also known as Waterbug, Venomous Bear, or Snake) is one of the world's longest-running state-sponsored APT groups. Attributed to Russia's Federal Security Service (FSB), Turla has exploited victims in over 50 countries since at least 2004. The group is known for its extremely sophisticated malware and innovative stealth techniques, including satellite-based command and control infrastructure.

The group targets government agencies, diplomatic missions, military entities, research and educational facilities, critical infrastructure sectors, and media organizations.

## Activities and Tactics
Turla employs some of the most sophisticated tactics in the threat landscape:

### Primary Targets
- Government agencies
- Diplomatic missions
- Military organizations
- Research and educational institutions
- Critical infrastructure
- Media organizations
- Pharmaceutical companies

### Key Motivations
- Long-term intelligence gathering
- Exfiltration of sensitive documents
- Strategic access maintenance
- Economic espionage

### Geographic Focus
- Europe (primary)
- North America
- Asia
- Middle East

## Notable Campaigns
1. **Agent.BTZ (2008)**: USB-based malware that spread through US military networks
2. **Satellite C2 Operations**: Using compromised satellite infrastructure for C2
3. **Watering Hole Campaigns**: Compromising government and research websites
4. **Snake Malware Operation**: Multi-year campaign using the Snake rootkit

## Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures (TTPs)
Turla is known for the following TTPs:

### Initial Access
- **Spear Phishing**: Targeted email campaigns with malicious documents
- **Drive-by Compromise**: Watering hole attacks on legitimate websites
- **Exploit Public-Facing Applications**: Exploiting vulnerable services

### Execution
- **PowerShell**: Extensive use for execution
- **Windows Management Instrumentation (WMI)**
- **msiexec**: For executing malicious MSI packages

### Persistence
- **Kernel Rootkits**: Snake rootkit for persistence
- **Scheduled Tasks**: Creating scheduled tasks for persistence

### Defense Evasion
- **Rootkits**: Kernel-level rootkits for stealth
- **Living off the Land**: Using legitimate Windows binaries

### Discovery
- **System Information Discovery**: Enumeration of systems
- **Account Discovery**: Identifying user accounts
- **File and Directory Discovery**

### Lateral Movement
- **PsExec**: Remote execution
- **Pass the Hash**: Credential-based movement

### Collection
- **Audio Capture**: collecting audio via microphones
- **Input Capture**: Keylogging and credential theft
- **Screen Capture**

### Exfiltration
- **Exfiltration Over Alternative Protocol**: Email-based exfiltration
- **Exfiltration Over C2 Channel**

## Notable Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)
Based on MITRE ATT&CK and security vendor reporting:

### Known Infrastructure
- **Satellite-Based C2**: Using compromised satellite earth stations
- **Hijacked Websites**: Using legitimate websites for C2

### MITRE ATT&CK Techniques
Turla uses techniques including:
- T1566.001 - Phishing: Spearphishing Attachment
- T1189 - Drive-by Compromise
- T1059.001 - PowerShell
- T1543.003 - Windows Service
- T1071.001 - Application Layer Protocol: DNS
- T1048 - Exfiltration Over Alternative Protocol

## Malware and Tools
Turla uses a range of highly sophisticated custom malware:

### Flagship Malware
- **Snake (Uroborough)**: Windows kernel rootkit, one of the most sophisticated malware ever analyzed
- **HyperStack**: Advanced backdoor combining multiple capabilities

### Backdoors
- **Carbon/Gazer**: Modular second-stage backdoor
- **Kazuar**: .NET-based multi-platform backdoor

### Additional Tools
- **Tunneller**: Network tunneling tool
- **SnakeKiller**: Tool to remove Snake infections
