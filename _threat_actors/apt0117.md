## Introduction
Fox Kitten (also known as Pioneer Kitten, Parisite, or Lemon Sandstorm) is a threat actor with a suspected nexus to the Iranian government that has been active since at least 2017. The group targets entities in the Middle East, North Africa, Europe, Australia, and North America.

Fox Kitten has targeted multiple industrial verticals including oil and gas, technology, government, defense, healthcare, manufacturing, and engineering.

## Activities and Tactics
Fox Kitten employs various cyber attack tactics:

### Primary Targets
- Oil and gas sector
- Technology companies
- Government organizations
- Defense contractors
- Healthcare organizations
- Manufacturing sectors

### Key Motivations
- Intellectual property theft
- Strategic access
- Geopolitical intelligence

### Geographic Focus
- Middle East (primary)
- North Africa
- Europe
- Australia
- North America

## Notable Campaigns
1. **Oil and Gas Targeting**: Ongoing attacks on energy sector
2. **Technology Sector Attacks**: Targeting technology companies
3. **Defense Contractor Operations**: Accessing defense-related information

## Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures (TTPs)
Fox Kitten is known for the following TTPs:

### Initial Access
- **Spear Phishing**: Targeted email campaigns
- **Exploit Public-Facing Applications**: Using vulnerabilities

### Execution
- **Command and Scripting Interpreter**: Using various interpreters
- **PowerShell**: For execution

### Persistence
- **Scheduled Tasks**: Creating scheduled tasks

### Defense Evasion
- **Obfuscation**: Hiding malicious activity

### Discovery
- **System Information Discovery**

### Lateral Movement
- **Remote Services**

### Collection
- **Data from Information Repositories**
